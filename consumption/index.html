Comparison of consumption-based emissions across countries

While most international reporting is of territorial emissions, there are other accounting methods for national emissions. Consumption-based emissions is one of these, and it attempts to represent the emissions associated with a country's economic consumption, no matter where those emissions occur in the world. For example, if someone in country X purchases a car that was manufactured in country Y, then the emissions in country Y associated with that manufacture would be re-allocated to country X, where the car was purchased.

Here I compare consumption emissions estimates from a number of different datasets. Most datasets do not cover all countries, and those that do make various assumptions due to lack of data. Your take-away message is that these estimates are highly uncertain.

Note that both Eora and EXIOBASE results include 'nowcasting' results (projections).

Data sources are:

  • The Eora Global Supply Chain Database (worldmrio.com/)
